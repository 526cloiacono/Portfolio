Before High School

Before high school, life was simple in the best way. I was a little kid running around with a glove, playing baseball almost every day, and just enjoying being outside. Most of my time was split between school, baseball, and being with my family — my mom, dad, and brother — who were always part of everything I did.

Early Life

Baseball Was Everything

Baseball was the biggest part of my childhood. I played third base, which always kept me locked in every play. I liked the pressure of it — fast ground balls, quick throws, and having to stay focused every pitch. It taught me how to react fast and not overthink things.

Family Always There

My mom, dad, and brother were always around supporting me. Whether it was games, practices, or just normal everyday life, they were part of everything. Some of my best memories are simple ones — talking after games, traveling, and just laughing at random moments.

Just a Kid Having Fun

Before high school I was just a funny, energetic kid who liked being outside and staying busy. I didn’t really think about the future — I just played baseball, hung out with friends, and tried to enjoy everything while it was happening.
